# Road Accident Analysis Dashboard

## 📊 Project Overview
This project features a comprehensive Road Accident Analysis dashboard designed to visualize and analyze traffic casualty data across the United Kingdom. By examining key metrics such as accident severity, vehicle types, road conditions, and geographical distribution, this dashboard identifies critical patterns in road safety.

The primary goal is to provide stakeholders with actionable insights into where and why accidents occur, enabling data-driven decisions for urban planning, traffic law enforcement, and public safety campaigns.

## 🛠️ Tools Used
- Power BI (Data Visualization & Dashboard Design)
- Power Query (Data Cleaning and Transformation)
- DAX (Custom Measures & KPI Calculations)

## 🔍 Problem, Action & Result
- The Problem: Raw accident data was fragmented and difficult to interpret, making it a challenge for safety officials to identify whether road safety measures were effectively reducing casualties year-over-year or which road types posed the highest risks.

- The Action: I imported and transformed the dataset using Power Query to ensure data consistency. I developed complex DAX measures to calculate Year-to-Date (YTD) casualties and Year-over-Year (YoY) growth rates. I then designed an interactive interface featuring a monthly trend line, a geographic map for hotspot identification, and categorized breakdowns by vehicle and road type.

- The Result: The analysis revealed a significant 11.9% overall decline in total casualties compared to the previous year. Specifically, it highlighted that Single Carriageways are the highest-risk road types and that Cars account for the vast majority of casualties (over 155k), allowing for more targeted safety interventions.
- 
## 💡 Data Insights
- Casualty Severity: While total casualties reached 195,737, "Slight" casualties make up the bulk of the data (165,837). However, Fatal casualties saw the most significant decrease at -33.3% YoY, indicating improvements in emergency response or vehicle safety.
- Urban vs. Rural: Urban areas account for the majority of accidents (61.95%), suggesting that high-density traffic and intersections remain the primary areas of concern compared to rural settings.
- Environmental Impact: Roughly 73.8% of accidents occur during daylight, challenging the assumption that poor lighting is the lead cause of incidents and pointing instead toward high-traffic volume periods.
- Vehicle Specifics: Cars are involved in the highest number of casualties (155,804), while Agricultural vehicles represent the lowest (399), providing a clear hierarchy of which transport sectors require the most rigorous safety standards.
